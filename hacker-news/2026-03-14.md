# Hacker News Summary - 2026-03-14

**[Can I run AI locally?]**
- **Summary:** CanIRun.ai is a new web-based tool that detects a user's hardware (GPU, CPU, RAM) via browser APIs and estimates which AI models can run locally. It provides performance estimates (tokens per second) for various quantized models.
- **Discussion:** Users debated the accuracy of the tool's estimations, particularly for Mixture of Experts (MoE) models where active parameters differ from total parameters. The discussion highlighted a shift towards small, efficient models like Qwen 3.5 9B for local tool use, while frontier models remain preferred for complex coding. There were also technical deep dives into unified memory architecture on Apple Silicon vs. AMD Strix Halo's dynamic VRAM allocation.
- **Links:** [Article](https://www.canirun.ai/) | [HN Thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47363754)

**[Qatar helium shutdown puts chip supply chain on a two-week clock]**
- **Summary:** A shutdown at Qatar's Ras Laffan helium plant, which accounts for 30% of global supply, has raised concerns about the semiconductor supply chain. SK Hynix and TSMC are monitoring the situation, though they claim to have diversified supplies for now.
- **Discussion:** The thread provided intense technical insight into why helium is critical for chip fabrication, specifically for wafer cooling and EUV lithography optics where ultra-pure (Grade 5/6) gas is required. Participants discussed the difficulty of recycling helium at such high purity levels and the fragility of "just-in-time" supply chains. A side discussion emerged regarding the US divesting from its strategic helium reserve.
- **Links:** [Article](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/qatar-helium-shutdown-puts-chip-supply-chain-on-a-two-week-clock) | [HN Thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47363584)

**[John Carmack about open source and anti-AI activists]**
- **Summary:** John Carmack shared his perspective on the intersection of open source software and AI training, arguing that open-sourcing code is a "gift" and that maintainers should not restrict how that gift is used, including for LLM training.
- **Discussion:** The community sparred over the definition of "Open Source," distinguishing between "code dumps" (releasing finished source) and active community development models (like the Linux kernel). Many argued that Carmack's "gift" analogy ignores the "strings" attached to copyleft licenses like the GPL, while others supported the idea that once code is public, its utility in training models is a net benefit to progress.

**[Show HN: Channel Surfer – Watch YouTube like it’s cable TV]**
- **Summary:** Channel Surfer is a web app that recreates a retro cable TV experience for YouTube. It features a curated grid of channels with a grainy interlace effect and a classic blue cable guide, allowing users to "surf" through topical content without the standard recommendation algorithm.
- **Discussion:** The project received high praise for its curation and nostalgia factor. Users discussed the "decision paralysis" caused by modern on-demand streaming and why the "lean-back" TV model is still psychologically satisfying. Technical questions focused on the CSS implementation of the CRT effect and the potential for a Roku or Android TV port.
- **Links:** [Article](https://channelsurfer.tv) | [HN Thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47336100)

**[Your phone is an entire computer]**
