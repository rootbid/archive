# Hacker News Summary - 2026-03-09

**[Ask HN: How to be alone?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47296547)**
- **Summary:** A 38-year-old individual seeks advice on transitioning to solitary living after a 20-year relationship. The post highlights the psychological difficulty of "solitary confinement with internet" and the hollow feeling of daily tasks without a partner.
- **Discussion:** Community members emphasized the necessity of routine, joining high-frequency social groups like gyms or churches, and the concept of "lingering" to allow for spontaneous social interactions. Technical insights touched on the neuroplasticity of habit formation and the distinction between situational and clinical depression.

**[Agent Safehouse – macOS-native sandboxing for local agents](https://agent-safehouse.dev/)** ([HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47301085))
- **Summary:** A security tool leveraging macOS `sandbox-exec` to create isolated environments for AI agents without the overhead of Docker or VMs. It provides a policy generator to grant minimal permissions for tasks like file access and shell execution.
- **Discussion:** Debates centered on the security of `sandbox-exec` (deprecated but still effective) versus containerization. Users discussed fine-grained process control and the challenges of agents accessing global configurations (e.g., `.gitconfig`) while maintaining strict filesystem boundaries.

**[Why can't you tune your guitar? (2019)](https://www.ethanhein.com/wp/2019/why-cant-you-tune-your-guitar/)** ([HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47254896))
- **Summary:** An analysis of the mathematical and physical limitations of 12-tone equal temperament (12-TET) on fretted instruments. It explains why certain intervals will always sound slightly out of tune due to the physics of vibrating strings.
- **Discussion:** Commenters explored "sweetened" tunings (e.g., James Taylor's), True Temperament frets, and the role of harmonics in instrument timbre. Technical discussions also addressed rolling shutter artifacts in string vibration videos and bit-banging audio on microcontrollers.

**[Microscopes can see video on a laserdisc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qZuR-772cks)** ([HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47291876))
- **Summary:** A demonstration showing that the analog video signal on LaserDiscs is physically discernible under high magnification. Constant Angular Velocity (CAV) discs allow vertical credits and patterns to align spatially on the disc surface.
- **Discussion:** Experts clarified the analog nature of LaserDiscs (encoding signal values as pit lengths) versus digital CDs. The conversation focused on signal processing, the impact of RPM on data density, and the serendipitous alignment of scrolling credits in microscopy.

**[Notes on writing Rust-based Wasm](https://notes.brooklynzelenka.com/Blog/Notes-on-Writing-Wasm)** ([HN Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47295837))

- **Discussion:** The debate centered on the Wasm Component Model and the performance overhead of "JS glue code." Technical insights included the trade-offs of Wasm Garbage Collection (GC) support and the necessity of executor-independence in library design.
