# Daily Market Sentiment Report: February 28, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary
The Indian stock market is expected to remain under pressure today, following a sharp selloff on Friday (Feb 27) where the Sensex plunged over 960 points and Nifty settled near 25,179. Sentiment is weighed down by weak global cues, particularly from Wall Street where risk-off sentiment prevailed due to geopolitical tensions and a lukewarm reaction to Nvidia's earnings. Asian markets have opened lower this morning, mirroring the US trend.

## 2. Indian Market Performance (Previous Close - Feb 27)
- **Indices:**
  - **Sensex:** Closed down ~961 points (-1.17%) at approx. 81,287.
  - **Nifty 50:** Ended lower by 317 points (-1.25%) at 25,179.
- **Key Drivers:**
  - **FII Outflows:** Continued selling by foreign institutional investors.
  - **GDP Jitters:** Caution ahead of the domestic quarterly GDP data release.
  - **Sectoral Impact:** selling was broad-based, with heavyweights in **Banking, Auto, and FMCG** dragging the indices. **IT stocks** showed some initial resilience but were eventually caught in the broader tech selloff.

## 3. Global Cues & Sentiment
- **US Markets:** Wall Street closed lower on Friday. Traders moved away from riskier assets citing geopolitical concerns (specifically US-Iran tensions) and worries over private credit markets. Tech stocks struggled as Nvidia's earnings guidance, though strong ($78bn Q1 revenue projection), failed to satisfy heightened investor expectations.
- **Asian Markets:** Early trade on Saturday morning (Feb 28) shows Asian equities tracking US losses. Despite this immediate dip, the region is poised to record its best February performance ever, driven by the broader AI infrastructure boom.
- **Geopolitics:** Escalating tensions between the US and Iran are adding a risk premium to crude oil prices and dampening equity sentiment.

## 4. Outlook for Today
- **Sentiment:** **Bearish / Cautious.**
- **Watchlist:**
  - **Tech Stocks:** May face continued pressure following the US tech slide.
  - **Oil & Gas:** Reacting to rising crude prices amidst geopolitical flare-ups.
  - **Banking:** Will likely dictate the trend for Nifty Bank after yesterday's drag.
- **Technical Levels:** Nifty has slipped below the crucial 25,300 mark. Sustained trading below this level could invite further downside towards 25,000.
