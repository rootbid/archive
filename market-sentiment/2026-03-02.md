# Market Sentiment Report: March 2, 2026

## 🌍 Global Cues & Geopolitics
**Sentiment: Risk-Off / Negative**
Global markets are currently dominated by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Reports of US strikes and damage to Dubai airport infrastructure have triggered a "risk-off" sentiment across asset classes.
*   **Conflict:** Israel-Iran tensions have intensified, with direct implications for global energy supply routes.
*   **Oil Prices:** Crude oil is surging, reportedly up ~20% year-to-date and eyeing the $80/barrel mark, acting as a major headwind for net-importing economies like India.
*   **US Markets:** Expectation of weak cues from US futures as investors flock to safe havens (Gold, Bonds) amidst the conflict.

## 🇮🇳 Indian Market Outlook
**Sentiment: Bearish to Sideways**
The domestic market is expected to open cautiously with a negative bias. The prevailing strategy remains "sell on rise" as Nifty struggles to reclaim higher zones.
*   **Nifty 50:** Likely to trade in a tight range with a bearish undertone.
    *   **Resistance:** 25,350 – 25,500 (Strong supply zone)
    *   **Support:** 25,100 (Immediate key level to watch)
*   **Bank Nifty:** Consolidating near 60,500. Weakness in global banking stocks or rising bond yields could pressure this index further.
*   **Institutional Flows:**
    *   **FIIs:** Invested significantly (~Rs 22,615cr) in February, but flows are expected to turn tentative or negative due to geopolitical risks.
    *   **DIIs:** Continues to provide a cushion, absorbing FII selling, but may not be enough to trigger a breakout.

## 📊 Key Drivers to Watch
1.  **Crude Oil Volatility:** Any further spike above $80/bbl will negatively impact OMCs (Oil Marketing Companies) and paints/tyres sectors.
2.  **Fiscal Data:** India’s fiscal deficit for April-Jan stood at ~63% of the full-year estimate ($107.69B), leaving some room for spending but maintaining fiscal discipline focus.
3.  **Sectoral Focus:**
    *   **Defensive:** Pharma and IT might see interest as safe havens.
    *   **Weakness:** Banking and high-beta infra stocks may face selling pressure.
