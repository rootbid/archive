# Daily Market Sentiment Report: March 10, 2026

## Global Market Overview
Global markets are experiencing extreme volatility driven by the ongoing US-Israeli conflict with Iran, which is now in its tenth day. The geopolitical tension has pushed crude oil prices above $100 per barrel, raising fears of supply chain disruptions and persistent global inflation. As a result, US and European equity indices, along with government bonds, have faced massive sell-offs over the past week, although Wall Street saw a slight rebound late Monday on tentative hopes that the conflict may be nearing a resolution. A strengthening US dollar and rising jet fuel costs continue to pressure specific sectors like global airlines and discretionary spending.

## Indian Market Impact
The Indian stock market has taken a severe beating due to these global headwinds. Key highlights include:
*   **Massive Sell-offs:** The Sensex and Nifty have plunged nearly 3% in recent sessions, with the Sensex shedding over 1,350 to 2,300+ points and the Nifty 50 dropping over 9% from its recent highs.
*   **Macro-Economic Risks:** Surging crude oil prices are a major threat to India, weighing heavily on investor sentiment due to the potential widening of India's twin deficits, an upward shift in the inflation trajectory, and potential hawkish pivots by the RBI.
*   **Sectoral Performance:** Broad-based selling has gripped Indian equities. Banking, auto, PSU banks, IT, and FMCG stocks are leading the losses, signaling a distinct risk-off sentiment among both foreign and domestic investors.
