# Market Sentiment Report: March 1, 2026

## Executive Summary
Global and Indian markets are entering the first week of March 2026 with a distinct **risk-off** sentiment. Following a sharp selloff on Wall Street on Friday, triggered by hotter-than-expected inflation data, Indian indices (Nifty/Sensex) are expected to open under pressure. The Nifty 50 closed the previous week near 25,178, shedding nearly 400 points, signaling a bearish trend amid sustained Foreign Institutional Investor (FII) outflows.

## Indian Market Outlook
*   **Trend:** Bearish to Cautious. The Nifty 50 has shown weakness, closing the week at 25,178.65.
*   **Key Levels:** Immediate support is seen around the 25,140 zone. A breach could lead to further downside.
*   **Valuation:** Market analysts note a compression in India's valuation premium relative to emerging markets (EM), dropping from ~80% in late 2024 to ~47%, potentially making it more attractive for long-term entry if global headwinds stabilize.
*   **FII Activity:** Continued selling by foreign investors remains a primary drag on the market, exacerbated by rising US bond yields and a strengthening dollar.

## Global Cues
*   **US Markets:** Wall Street faced significant selling pressure on Friday, February 27. The **Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged over 500 points (~1.05%)**, closing below the 49,000 mark.
*   **Inflation Fears:** The catalyst was a stronger-than-expected Producer Price Index (PPI) report showing a 0.5% rise in January 2026, reigniting fears that the Federal Reserve may delay rate cuts or keep rates higher for longer.
*   **Tech Sector:** A "tech selloff" and "AI anxiety" were cited as contributing factors, weighing heavily on the Nasdaq and S&P 500.

## Key Drivers to Watch
1.  **US Inflation Data:** The hot PPI reading has shifted sentiment; markets will be hyper-sensitive to any further economic data this week.
2.  **FII Flows:** The trajectory of foreign outflows from Indian equities will dictate the short-term trend.
3.  **Global Tech Performance:** With US tech stocks correcting, Indian IT majors may face headwinds on Monday.
