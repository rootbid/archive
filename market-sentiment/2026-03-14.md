# Daily Market Sentiment Report - March 14, 2026

## Global Market Overview
Global markets are experiencing elevated volatility and risk-off sentiment. Following the recent US-Iran geopolitical tensions and the associated oil price shocks, major US indexes have taken a hit. The S&P 500 reached a new low for 2026, marking a 1.6% loss for the week and notching its first three-week losing streak in roughly a year. The Dow Jones slid roughly 2% on the week, reflecting broad investor anxiety over macroeconomic stability and persistent global conflict. 

## Indian Market Impact
The Indian stock market has faced a severe meltdown, tracking weak global cues and the surging price of crude oil. 
- **Benchmarks Tumble**: At the latest close, the BSE Sensex plummeted by approximately 1,470 points (1.93%) to 74,563, while the NSE Nifty50 fell by 488 points (2.06%) to 23,151.
- **Wealth Erosion**: Over Rs 9.5 lakh crore in investor wealth was wiped out in recent sessions.
- **Broader Trend**: The Sensex has lost roughly 6,000 points (about 7.5%) in a little over two weeks since the geopolitical escalation began in late February.
- **Key Drivers**: Escalating tensions in the Middle East involving Iran, Israel, and the US, combined with subsequent oil supply fears, are the primary headwinds. Higher oil prices directly threaten India's inflation outlook and current account deficit.

## Outlook
Volatility is expected to remain high in the near term. Investors are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, global oil prices, and foreign institutional capital flows to gauge whether the current correction represents a buying opportunity or the beginning of a deeper bear market phase.
