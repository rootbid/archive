# Daily Market Sentiment Report: March 11, 2026

## Global Market Overview
Global markets have experienced extreme volatility recently, primarily driven by the escalating US-Israeli war with Iran. The conflict caused a massive surge in crude oil prices due to supply fears, prompting sharp selloffs in government bonds, European stocks, and Wall Street indices. However, markets witnessed a rebound early this week. US stocks ended higher and European markets opened stronger on Tuesday after US President Donald Trump signaled that the conflict in Iran might be nearing its end, leading to a slight cool-off in oil prices. Despite the recovery, major figures like the CEO of Saudi Aramco have warned of a "drastic domino effect" on global industries, particularly aviation and automotive, due to the sustained elevation in fuel costs.

## Indian Market Impact
The Indian stock market closely mirrored global turbulence. Last week and earlier this week, benchmark indices plummeted, with the Sensex tanking over 1,350 points and the Nifty 50 dropping over 1,000 points (down around 9% from recent highs). The fall was heavily influenced by boiling crude oil prices, geopolitical tensions, and continuous foreign fund outflows. IT and FMCG stocks led the losses, along with crude-sensitive sectors like paints.

However, a significant reversal occurred on Tuesday, March 10. Both the Sensex and Nifty 50 rebounded nearly 1% (Sensex settling 640 points higher, Nifty closing above 24,250). This recovery was fueled by improving global cues—specifically the drop in oil prices following comments regarding the potential de-escalation of the Middle East conflict. Midcap and smallcap indices also outperformed during this rebound.

## Key Drivers to Watch
- **Geopolitical Developments:** Any escalation or definitive end to the US-Iran conflict.
- **Crude Oil Prices:** Highly sensitive to the ongoing war; directly impacts India's import bills and inflation.
- **Foreign Institutional Investor (FII) Flows:** Outflows have pressured Indian markets; a sustained global recovery might reverse this trend.
