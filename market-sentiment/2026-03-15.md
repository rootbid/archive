# Market Sentiment Report: March 15, 2026

## Global Market Sentiment
Global stock markets are currently experiencing a state of **Extreme Fear** (Fear and Greed Index: 20), driven primarily by an ongoing Middle Eastern conflict involving the US, Israel, and Iran. This geopolitical instability has triggered a severe oil shock, with US crude topping $87. Saudi Aramco's CEO has warned of "catastrophic consequences" and a "drastic domino effect" impacting global industries like aviation, agriculture, and shipping. Consequently, inflation fears have resurged, leading markets to price in only a single Federal Reserve interest rate cut for the entire year.

## Indian Market Impact
The Indian stock market has suffered a major bloodbath following the global cues, particularly the oil shock. Benchmark indices have faced their steepest weekly fall in over a year.
*   **BSE Sensex:** Plummeted by roughly 1,470 points to close around 74,563.
*   **NSE Nifty 50:** Tumbled nearly 488 points to settle near 23,151.
*   **Wealth Erosion:** Approximately Rs 9.5 Lakh Crore in investor wealth has been wiped out as investors remain highly cautious. Technical indicators like the RSI suggest the index is in an oversold zone, pointing to sustained bearish momentum with immediate support around the 22,500 level.

## Key Themes to Watch
1. **Oil Prices:** Any prolonged escalation in the Middle East could further spike crude prices, disproportionately affecting India's import bill and inflation.
2. **Geopolitical Tensions:** The US-Israel-Iran conflict and associated disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz are dictating risk appetite.
3. **Federal Reserve Policy:** Re-accelerating inflation driven by energy costs has dashed hopes for near-term rate cuts, pressuring global liquidity and emerging market equities.
4. **Clean Energy:** With traditional energy prices surging, clean energy stocks and funds (e.g., iShares Global Clean Energy ETF) are seeing renewed investor interest as an alternative.
