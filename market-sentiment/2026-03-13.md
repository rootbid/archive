# Daily Market Sentiment Report - 2026-03-13

## 1. Indian Market Sentiment
The Indian stock market has experienced significant volatility this week, primarily driven by external geopolitical pressures rather than domestic fundamentals. 
- **Major Indices:** The Sensex saw heavy selling pressure earlier, tumbling over 1,300 points, before staging a sharp recovery. Recently, the Sensex rebounded by approximately 750 points from its lows to trade around the 76,500 level. The Nifty 50 mirrored this volatility, briefly slipping below 23,650 before reclaiming the 23,800 mark.
- **Key Drivers:** The primary catalyst for the widespread risk-off sentiment in India is the escalating tension involving Iran, which has sharply spooked investors and driven up energy costs, heavily impacting India's oil-import-dependent economy.

## 2. Global Market Sentiment
Global sentiment is cautious, balancing robust energy sector performance against looming macroeconomic headwinds and geopolitical flashpoints.
- **Energy Sector Surge:** Geopolitical tensions, particularly involving the Strait of Hormuz and Iran, have pushed ICE Brent crude near the $100 per barrel mark. This has led to global energy ETFs and heavyweights like Occidental Petroleum and Shell hitting new highs.
- **Recession Outlook:** Major institutions maintain a cautious macroeconomic outlook. J.P. Morgan Global Research currently flags a 35% probability of a U.S. and global recession in 2026, driven by a projected downshift in consumption.

## 3. Impact on the Indian Market
The combination of $100/barrel crude oil and global recessionary fears puts a dual pressure on Indian equities. Rising energy costs threaten to widen the current account deficit and stoke inflation, limiting the RBI's maneuverability. Meanwhile, defensive strategies appear to be taking priority as investors limit fresh investments in select overseas schemes and trim exposure in high-beta sectors.
