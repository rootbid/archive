# Daily Market Sentiment Report: March 22, 2026

## Global Market Sentiment
Global equity markets are experiencing severe downward pressure, largely driven by the ongoing US-Iran conflict which began in late February 2026. The geopolitical turmoil has sparked fears of a worsening energy shock, driving up crude oil prices and reigniting global inflation concerns. 
- **Major Indices:** Since the conflict began, the S&P 500 has lost approximately 5.4%, the Nasdaq has declined 4.5%, and the Dow is down nearly 7%.
- **Recession & Rate Cut Fears:** Rising oil prices have caused investors to dial back expectations for central bank rate cuts. Bond yields are climbing as inflation fears mount, leading to a third consecutive weekly decline for global shares. 
- **Correction Territory:** At their lowest points this week, both the Dow and Nasdaq traded near correction territory, underscoring broad market vulnerability.

## Indian Market Sentiment
The Indian stock market sentiment mirrors the fragile global environment, heavily impacted by "war-related uncertainties" and the resulting surge in crude oil prices.
- **Recent Volatility:** On March 19th, Indian markets suffered a massive crash, with the Sensex plummeting around 2,500 points (3.6%) to 73,950.95 and the Nifty 50 dropping 847 points (3.5%) to 22,930.35. This single-day drop wiped out an estimated ₹12 lakh crore in investor wealth.
- **Modest Recovery but Fragile Outlook:** Markets saw a slight dead-cat bounce or stabilization on March 20th, with both the Nifty50 and Sensex closing roughly 0.5% higher (Nifty above 23,100; Sensex above 74,500). Despite this, investor sentiment remains highly fragile. Traders are reluctant to hold long positions over the weekend given the unpredictable nature of the Middle East conflict.
- **Key Domestic Risks:** As a major oil importer, India faces acute risks from the energy shock. Elevated crude prices threaten to widen the current account deficit, weaken the Rupee, and stall the RBI's ability to cut domestic interest rates.
