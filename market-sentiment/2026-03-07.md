# Market Sentiment Report: March 7, 2026

**Date:** Saturday, March 7, 2026  
**Status:** Weekend Review (Post-Friday Close)

## 📉 Executive Summary
Global equity markets ended the week on a significantly bearish note, driven by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East (specifically US-Israel-Iran conflicts) and a subsequent surge in crude oil prices. Indian markets mirrored this negative sentiment, suffering sharp losses on Friday, March 6.

## 🇮🇳 Indian Market Performance (Friday Close)
*   **Sensex:** Closed at **78,918**, down **1,097 points (-1.37%)**.
*   **Nifty 50:** Closed at **24,450**, down **315 points** (breaching the crucial 24,500 support level).
*   **Sectoral View:** Sharp sell-off observed across most sectors, with oil marketing companies (OMCs) particularly vulnerable due to rising feedstock costs.
*   **Key Drivers:**
    *   **Geopolitics:** Escalating hostilities involving Iran have fueled fears of an energy supply shock.
    *   **Oil Prices:** Brent crude surged to ~$87.57 per barrel, triggering inflation fears and margin compression concerns for Indian corporate earnings.
    *   **Foreign Flows:** Global risk-off sentiment likely triggered FII outflows.

## 🌎 Global Market Context
*   **US Markets (Friday Close):**
    *   **Dow Jones:** Fell **453.19 points (-0.95%)** to 47,501.55.
    *   **S&P 500:** Dropped **1.33%** to 6,740.02.
    *   **Nasdaq:** Declined **1.59%**.
    *   *Note:* The Dow recovered partially from an intraday low of nearly -950 points, suggesting some dip-buying volatility, but the trend remains bearish.
*   **Commodities:** Oil prices are the central focus, with Brent crude climbing significantly (+16% YTD).

## 🔮 Outlook for Monday (March 9)
*   **Sentiment:** **Bearish / Volatile**.
*   **Watchlist:**
    *   **Crude Oil:** Any further spike over the weekend will severely impact Monday's opening.
    *   **Geopolitics:** Weekend developments in the Middle East will be the primary market driver.
    *   **Tech Sector:** US tech weakness (Nasdaq -1.59%) often correlates with pressure on Indian IT stocks.
